🎯 Behavioral & Leadership Interview Guide

A comprehensive preparation guide for FAANG behavioral interviews — covering the STAR method, leadership principles, product sense, and how to structure compelling stories that demonstrate impact.

📍 Roadmap Overview​

Recommended path: 1 → 2 → 3 → 4

Unlike technical sections, behavioral prep requires active story crafting — reading alone is not enough.

🟡 Chapter 1 — Behavioral Interviews​

Goal: Master the STAR framework and prepare 6–8 versatile stories mapped to common interview themes.

📖 Read: 01 — Behavioral Interviews

  • Structure answers using STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
  • Prepare stories covering: conflict, failure, leadership, ambiguity, impact
  • Quantify results with metrics and business impact
  • Practice concise delivery (2–3 minutes per story)
  • Handle follow-up probing questions
  • Avoid common pitfalls (vague answers, blaming others, no metrics)

🟡 Chapter 2 — Leadership Principles​

Goal: Map your experiences to FAANG leadership values and demonstrate culture fit.

📖 Read: 02 — Leadership Principles

  • Know Amazon's 16 Leadership Principles with examples
  • Understand Google's "Googleyness" values
  • Map Meta's core values to your experiences
  • Demonstrate ownership and customer obsession
  • Show bias for action with calculated risk-taking
  • Explain earn trust and disagree-and-commit scenarios
  • Prepare cross-functional collaboration stories

🟡 Chapter 3 — Product Sense & Metrics​

Goal: Think like a product engineer — define success metrics, reason about trade-offs, and design for users.

📖 Read: 03 — Product Sense

  • Define success metrics for a product feature (DAU, retention, engagement)
  • Design A/B tests with proper hypothesis and sample sizing
  • Reason about user personas and use cases
  • Make product trade-off decisions with data
  • Propose 3 metrics to measure a new feature's success
  • Explain how engineering decisions impact product outcomes
  • Balance technical excellence with business value delivery

🟣 Chapter 4 — Common Interview Questions​

Goal: Practice the most common behavioral questions with structured frameworks.

📖 Read: 04 — Common Interview Questions

  • "Tell me about a time you disagreed with a technical decision"
  • "Describe a project that failed and what you learned"
  • "How do you handle competing priorities?"
  • "Tell me about a time you mentored someone"
  • "Describe your most impactful project"
  • "How do you handle ambiguity in requirements?"
